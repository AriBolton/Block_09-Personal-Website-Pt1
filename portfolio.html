Career Highlights

While I have yet to complete a website or mobile application, I do have many transferrable skills that I've learned over the years that I plan to apply in this next endeavor.

Project Assistant

My first job after graduating college was as a project assistant for a construction company. I learned many valuable skills, including how to manage projects from beginning to end. I learned the importance of scheduling, and thorough documentation. I also got a lot of practice in prioritizing, and juggling multiple tasks with competing deadlines.

Insurance Agent

After leaving the construction role, I went into Insurance for a bit. I learned many valuable skills there as well, including the importance of providing great customer service. As many of us know, it can be challenging when you're constantly dealing with people calling in who are upset with your product, or prices, etc. The more you interact with the customers, the easier it gets to manage their expectations, and to put them at ease with your knowledge, and ability to support them and make it right however possible.

Executive Assistant

My two roles prior to my current role of an exxecutive assistant, both prepared me for the job. While all of the jobs that I've had are different, they all have many things that overlap. As an executive assistant, I not only have to manage my own schedule and competing priorities, but also be sure that the schedules for all four leaders I support are in order. This role combines project management, customer service, and being an assistant all into one job. My goal on a daily basis is to manage priorities and schedules, and make sure that my customers (aka my bosses) are satisfied at the end of the day.
